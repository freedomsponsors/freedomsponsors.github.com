FreedomSponsors

Crowdfunding open source, one issue at a time

What is FreedomSponsors.

FreedomSponsors is a crowdfunding platform for open source projects, specifically, open source project's issues.

Anyone can go and place a bounty for an issue they need. If more people need it, thay can "chip in" and the bounties add up.

It's like JIRA's voting system, but the votes come with a "money offer" attached. Developers then can resolve the issues and claim their prizes.

The sponsors are notified and pay - if they're happy with the result.

You can think of FS as a big online board for WANTED signs like in the wild west movies :-)

FreedomSponsors is open source on Github (and help is welcome). Feel free to browse (or add issues to) our issue tracker or leave us some feedback.

How to use it.

Whether you want to place a bounty or begin working on issues, first you need to sign up. Currently, the only way to sign up is using a GMail account (but we'll add more methods soon).

Place new bounties by clicking on "Sponsor new issue", or join other sponsors on existing bounties by clicking "Sponsor this issue" on the issue page.

When placing a new bounty, you need to provide the "original" issue URL, like "https://hibernate.onjira.com/browse/HHH-1050". FS will try to discover a few issue details, like issue key, title and project - so you don't have to type those yourself. You also specify a few acceptance criteria:

  • requirements (free text),
  • expiration date,
  • "no forking" (checkbox), and
  • "require release" (checkbox).

When you finish placing your bounty, you get a popup reminder that you have to let the developers know about it. And the best way to do that is to visit the original issue URL and drop a comment with a link to the bounty you just created.

When a developer starts working on an issue, he can let everyone know by clicking "Work on this issue" (the developer will nedd a FS account too, of course). The sponsors are notified by email - and they are also notified when the developer clicks the "Resolve issue" button.

The sponsors can verify whether their problem is really gone, and pay if they're happy with the result. Or just don't otherwise.

This is important: there is nothing on the system that forces nobody to pay anything. There are no escrows and no pre paid credits - this is not an implementation detail - it's by philosophy.

The whole thing is voluntary to begin with. We want sponsors to pay the developers because they feel grateful for their work, and only after their problem is actually solved.

Payment.

Currently, the only supported payment method is Paypal. We charge a 3% fee out of the developer's pay (to cover hosting and other expenses).

We use Paypal's Parallel payment type. On a parallel payment, an account A can make a single payment that will be split to two or more receiver accounts.

Suppose a sponsor wants to pay his U$ 10 bounty to two developers, and the sponsor decides that she will give U$5 to each. On the payment page (on Paypal's website), the sponsor will see:

  - Developer A ......... U$ 4.85
  - Developer B ......... U$ 4.85
  - FREEDOMSPONSORS ..... U$ 0.30

Paypal's fees will also aplly to all three receivers.

Spreading the word

We believe that if this model really catches, it might have a huge positive impact on open source projects.

Developers will be able to spend more time working on the projects they love. Open bounties will make a gentle pressure on projects into making more frequent releases. It's an "everyone wins" world.

We believe we can make that happen.

We want to let the world know that FreedomSponsors is made by developers, for developers. Our biggest goal is to serve the open source community the best we can.

If you love open source too, and you believe that vision, then

Happy sponsoring!
